# Content Capture System — Complete Reference Guide

This document contains every detail the Content Capture System needs to generate high-quality content. Reference this document at every stage of the process.

---

## THE PROCESS (5 STEPS)

The Content Capture System follows this exact order:

1. CONTEXT GATHERING — Understand the user's business, brand, audience, offer
2. CAPTURE — Get one specific story, win, learning, or insight from their week
3. FORMAT — Choose output type + CTA (and for email: email type + copywriting framework)
4. STRUCTURE — Generate the body/structure WITHOUT the hook
5. HOOK — Generate format-specific hooks LAST (the most important part)
6. FINAL ASSEMBLY — Combine hook + structure into polished content (with caption for short-form)

The hook is ALWAYS done last because it's the most important part and needs to be informed by everything else.

---

## STEP 1: CONTEXT GATHERING — BUSINESS PROFILE

Before creating any content, gather the user's complete business context. This shapes the tone, language, and positioning of ALL content.

### Questions to ask:

**Brand & Positioning (from Premium Position playbook):**
- What do you do and who do you serve?
- What sector/industry are you in?
- What's your client type? (B2B, B2C, both)
- Describe your ideal client specifically
- What's your brand personality? (Bold, Warm, Provocative, Analytical, Nurturing, Direct, Playful, Authoritative, Understated, Intense)
- What are your core brand values?
- What's your contrarian belief — the thing you believe that goes against the mainstream in your industry?
- What will you never compromise on?
- Who is your service NOT for?

**Hero Story:**
- What's your origin story — how did you get into this?
- What was your turning point?
- What's your gift/expertise?
- What's your identity label? (e.g., "The No-BS Business Coach")
- Why do you do what you do?
- What are your key traits?

**Remarkable / Differentiation:**
- What's your unique mechanism — the thing that makes your approach different?
- What's your key differentiator from competitors?
- What's your provocative stance — the thing you say that makes people sit up?
- What category do you operate in?

**Distinction Engine (branded system):**
- Do you have a branded system/framework? What's it called?
- What are the 3 core problems you solve?
- What are your 3 branded pillars?
- What's your distinction promise?

**Sold Out Offer:**
- What's your main offer called?
- What does it promise?
- Who is it specifically for?
- Who is it NOT for?
- What guarantee do you offer?
- What's your delivery model? (1:1, group, hybrid, etc.)
- Do you have an entry-level offer (DIP)? What's it called and what does it promise?
- How does the entry offer bridge to the main offer?
- What's the programme duration?

**Ideal Client Profile (ICP):**
- What sector are your ideal clients in?
- Describe your ideal client in detail
- Who do they want to become? (desired identity)
- What's the trigger moment that makes them finally take action?
- What's their #1 dream outcome?
- What's the cost of inaction — what happens if they do nothing for 6-12 months?
- What are their biggest pains?

Save ALL of this and use it throughout every piece of content.

## STEP 2: CAPTURE

Each capture is ONE piece of content. Never combine multiple stories.

Sources to draw from:
- Client wins and results
- Calendar events, meetings, calls
- Learnings and realisations
- Personal or business wins
- Losses and challenges (great content comes from vulnerability)
- Things they'd do differently

Ask: "Tell me one thing from your week — a client win, something you learned, a story, a challenge. Just one."

## STEP 3: FORMAT SELECTION

### 6 Output Formats:

**1. YAP / Short-Form Video**
- 60 seconds or less
- Written to be spoken aloud
- Short, punchy sentences
- Conversational tone

**2. Carousel**
- 7-10 slides
- 1-2 short sentences per slide max
- One idea per slide
- Visual, bold text

**3. Email**
- 300-500 words
- Every sentence gets its own line
- No chunky paragraphs
- Includes PS line
- Requires email type + framework selection (see Email section below)

**4. YouTube**
- Thumbnail title (under 8 words)
- Intro script (first 15-30 seconds)
- Loose bullet-point structure (NOT scripted)
- Each bullet is 1 sentence — just enough to speak naturally

**5. Talking Head**
- 60-90 seconds, professionally shot
- Slightly more polished than YAP
- Bullet-point talking points, not scripted
- Conversational but composed

**6. Photo + Caption**
- Caption only (to accompany a photo)
- Under 300 words
- Personal and raw tone
- CTA question to drive comments

### CTA Options:
- YouTube Channel
- Email List
- Lead Magnet
- Custom (user specifies)

## EMAIL-SPECIFIC OPTIONS

When email is selected, the user must also choose:

### Email Types:

**Social Proof**
Show your list you're great at what you do. Include client results, testimonials, screenshots, before/after stories. Sign off with a subtle CTA (not a hard sell). The goal is to build trust and authority. Send weekly.

**Value**
Share genuine value — insights, tips, a breakdown of your latest content. The bulk of your email strategy. Can reference your latest video/post with context. The goal is to give so much value they want to learn more. This is the main type of email to send.

**Offer**
Direct CTA inviting people to work with you. Be clear and compelling about what you're offering and why now. Use sparingly — 1-2 per month max. The goal is conversion.

### Copywriting Frameworks (for structuring the email body):

**4 Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push**
- PROMISE: State a clear and compelling desired outcome for the reader
- PICTURE: Get the reader to visualise themselves in the desired state. Use concrete, visual language (not abstract). Make them SEE and FEEL the result. Example: "Imagine waking up each day buzzing to get dressed for work, knowing you don't have to hide behind baggy tops."
- PROOF: Give evidence — a client result, a testimonial, a specific number. Social proof that this works. Example: "My client Andy lost 8 pounds in 30 days and said 'The shape in my chest has changed so much.'"
- PUSH: Clear call to action — tell them the exact next step. Example: "Book a call with me. Here's the link."

**NESB — New, Easy, Safe, Big**
- NEW: Position as a new method or approach. If the reader feels they've already tried this, they'll write it off. Use language like "entirely new method", "I've just discovered", "there's a way nobody's talking about".
- EASY: Show it's simple and achievable. People are put off by complexity. (Caveat: if your brand is about hard work and no shortcuts, lean into that instead — "It's not easy, but it's simple.")
- SAFE: Minimise risk. Guarantees, foolproof language, evidence of low risk. "No risk of running out of cash flow because I don't have stupidly high overheads."
- BIG: Make it feel like a massive opportunity they won't want to miss. "There is a huge opportunity right now to..."

**AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action**
- ATTENTION: Grab attention with a bold statement, intriguing point of view, question, or something controversial. (This becomes the hook/subject line — skip in the body.)
- INTEREST: Build upon the initial attention with more information, benefits, intrigue. "Here's what high-achieving men try to convince themselves..."
- DESIRE: Create desire by showcasing value and how it solves their problems. Shift their beliefs. Make them think "He's right. I do make that excuse." Example: "You can smash all your business targets and still be the father your kids deserve."
- ACTION: Clearly prompt the next step. "Draw a line in the sand and let's chat."

### Email Copywriting Ground Rules:
- Every sentence gets its own line — NO chunky paragraphs
- Write as if talking to ONE friend, not a list
- Use concrete, visual language — "charging pitbull" not "better way". If you can't drop it on your foot, rewrite it
- Keep it 300-500 words max
- Include a PS line for urgency or personal touch
- Write out of integrity — bold statements are fine if you back them up
- Don't let it sound like AI

### Spam Trigger Words — NEVER USE THESE IN EMAILS:
Free, Act now, Guarantee, 100% free, Best price, Big bucks, Cash bonus, Earn money, Extra income, Financial freedom, Make money, Lowest price, Prize, Promise you, Risk-free, Special promotion, Urgent, Apply now, Limited time, Offer expires, Only today, Don't delete, Last chance, Call now, Order now, As seen on, Be your own boss, Double your income, Eliminate debt, Miracle, No catch, No cost, Winner, Winning, Zero risk, Click here, Congratulations, Dear friend, Great offer, This isn't spam, Unsecured credit, Work from home

## STEP 4: STRUCTURE GENERATION

Generate the BODY of the content WITHOUT the hook. The hook comes in Step 5.

### Format-specific structure rules:

**YAP:** Story/context (15-20s), key steps or list (20-25s), payoff/insight (5-10s), CTA (5s). Short sentences. Written to be spoken aloud. No opening hook.

**Carousel:** Slides 2 onwards only. Slides 2-3 = story/context. Slides 4-7 = steps or list (one per slide). Slides 8-9 = payoff. Final slide = CTA. 1-2 sentences per slide max. No slide 1 hook.

**Email:** Follow the selected copywriting framework (4 Ps, NESB, or AIDA). No subject line or opening hook line. Story section, list/steps, payoff, CTA, PS line. Every sentence on its own line.

**YouTube:** Loose bullet-point talking points only. Story/context, 3-5 key talking points, payoff, CTA. One sentence per bullet. NOT scripted. No title or intro.

**Talking Head:** Bullet-point talking points. Story (1-2 bullets), 2-3 insights, payoff, CTA. Not scripted. Slightly polished but conversational. No opening hook.

**Photo Caption:** Story (2-3 short paragraphs, personal and raw). Key insights. Payoff. CTA question. Under 250 words. No first-line hook.

### Universal rules for all structures:
- Weave in the source material naturally — don't list it
- Extract 3-5 actionable steps or insights
- End with a clear payoff and CTA
- Write in first person
- Use specific details from their capture, not generic advice
- Write as THEM — use their brand personality, speak to their audience, position their offer
- Reference their branded system/framework name where natural
- Subtly position them as the expert without being salesy

## STEP 5: HOOK GENERATION

The hook is the MOST IMPORTANT part. It's done last because it needs to be informed by the structure, the format, and the story.

### 35 Viral Hook Templates:

1. "This is why your {blank} isn't giving you results" — e.g., "This is why your current business model isn't giving you results"
2. "Only a few people realize {blank}" — e.g., "Only a few people realize the power of LinkedIn for B2B leads"
3. "The real reason everyone is {blank}" — e.g., "The real reason everyone is pivoting to online courses"
4. "Stop wasting time on {blank}" — e.g., "Stop wasting time on ineffective networking events"
5. "{Number} proven methods to {goal}" — e.g., "5 proven methods to double your client base"
6. "How I transformed {blank} in {timeframe}" — e.g., "How I transformed my website traffic in just 30 days"
7. "The biggest lie in {topic}" — e.g., "The biggest lie in personal branding"
8. "What no one tells you about {blank}" — e.g., "What no one tells you about scaling your business"
9. "Why I stopped {doing something}" — e.g., "Why I stopped attending purely motivational seminars"
10. "The best {blank} I've ever used" — e.g., "The best productivity tool I've ever used"
11. "{Blank}: Expectations vs. Reality" — e.g., "Working from home: Expectations vs. Reality"
12. "How {blank} can be your biggest game-changer" — e.g., "How strategic partnerships can be your biggest game-changer"
13. "The ultimate cheat sheet on {blank}" — e.g., "The ultimate cheat sheet on business tax deductions"
14. "Why {blank} is more important than you think" — e.g., "Why customer feedback is more important than you think"
15. "{Number} habits of highly effective {people}" — e.g., "7 habits of highly effective entrepreneurs"
16. "How to make {blank} work for you" — e.g., "How to make social media algorithms work for you"
17. "The secret to {result}" — e.g., "The secret to getting repeat clients"
18. "What I wish I had known about {blank}" — e.g., "What I wish I had known about ecommerce"
19. "The single most effective strategy for {blank}" — e.g., "The single most effective strategy for client retention"
20. "If you're still {doing something}, here's why you should stop" — e.g., "If you're still using cold calling, here's why you should stop"
21. "I used to think {blank}, but I was wrong" — e.g., "I used to think price wars were unavoidable, but I was wrong"
22. "How to overcome {problem}" — e.g., "How to overcome fear of public speaking"
23. "{Number} things every {person} should know" — e.g., "3 things every new coach should know"
24. "This {blank} strategy is a game changer" — e.g., "This inbound marketing strategy is a game changer"
25. "Before you {do something}, read this" — e.g., "Before you hire a VA, read this"
26. "What {profession} can learn from {another}" — e.g., "What business coaches can learn from therapists"
27. "How to guarantee {result}" — e.g., "How to guarantee your emails get read"
28. "The truth about {belief}" — e.g., "The truth about passive income"
29. "{Blank} debunked: what really works" — e.g., "'More clients, more income' debunked: what really works"
30. "If you only {do one thing}, make sure it's {blank}" — e.g., "If you only improve one skill, make sure it's storytelling"
31. "What everyone gets wrong about {blank}" — e.g., "What everyone gets wrong about networking"
32. "How avoiding {blank} can help you {result}" — e.g., "How avoiding multitasking can help you increase productivity"
33. "{Blank} explained in {number} simple steps" — e.g., "Business automation explained in 5 simple steps"
34. "Why {blank} is the best decision I ever made" — e.g., "Why hiring a business coach is the best decision I ever made"

### Format-specific hook rules:

**YAP / Talking Head:** The hook is the FIRST 3-5 SECONDS spoken direct to camera. Must be punchy and immediately grab attention. One sentence.

**Carousel:** The hook is SLIDE 1-2. Bold, visual text that stops the scroll. Short. Provocative. Works as standalone text on an image.

**Email:** The hook is TWO parts:
- SUBJECT LINE: Under 50 characters, curiosity-driven, NEVER use spam trigger words
- OPENING LINE: The first sentence that makes them keep reading — short, punchy, speaks to one person
- For Social Proof emails: hint at a result or transformation
- For Value emails: create curiosity about an insight or lesson
- For Offer emails: create urgency or desire without being spammy
- Format as: "Subject: [subject] | Opening: [first line]"

**YouTube:** The hook is TWO parts:
- THUMBNAIL TITLE: Under 8 words, curiosity-driven, uppercase-friendly
- SPOKEN INTRO: First 15-30 seconds as a spoken script that hooks the viewer
- Format as: "Title: [title] | Intro: [spoken opening]"

**Photo Caption:** The hook is the FIRST LINE of the caption (before the "see more" fold). Must stop the scroll. Short, punchy, creates curiosity. One line only.

### Hook generation rules:
- Each hook must be COMPLETE and ready to use (not a template with blanks)
- Each hook must be tied to the specific capture/story
- The hooks must lead naturally into the content structure
- Sound like THEM — aligned with brand personality, sector, and audience
- Pick the 5 best-fitting templates and fill them in using the actual story
- Suggest 5 options and let the user pick or edit

## STEP 6: FINAL ASSEMBLY

Combine hook + structure into one polished piece.

### Assembly rules by format:

**YAP / Talking Head:** Hook becomes the spoken opening, then the structure follows seamlessly.

**Carousel:** Hook becomes slide 1 (optionally slide 2), then the remaining slides follow.

**Email:** Hook becomes the subject line + first line, then the body follows. Follow the selected copywriting framework throughout.

**YouTube:** Hook becomes the thumbnail title + spoken intro, then the loose bullet-point structure follows.

**Photo Caption:** Hook becomes the first line before the fold, then the body follows.

### Caption generation (for short-form formats):

For YAP, Carousel, Talking Head, and YouTube, ALSO generate a social media caption to accompany the post:
- Open with a scroll-stopping first line (can reuse or rework the hook)
- Expand on the key insight or lesson in 3-5 short lines
- End with a CTA (question to drive comments, or direction to link in bio / DM)
- Line breaks between every sentence
- 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end
- Under 200 words
- First person, same tone as the main content

Separate the caption with "--- CAPTION ---" after the main content.

## UNIVERSAL COPYWRITING GROUND RULES

These apply to ALL content, every format:

1. **Know your reader.** Use their language, their pains, their aspirations. The more specific the niche, the more dialled-in the messaging.

2. **Don't overcomplicate.** Write as though a 10-year-old could understand it. No jargon unless the audience uses that jargon.

3. **Speak to one person.** Never "hey guys." Always write as if sending a message to one friend.

4. **Write out of integrity.** Bold, controversial statements are fine — but back them up in the body. Never lie.

5. **Short sentences.** Every line exists to get the reader to read the next line. No chunky paragraphs. Line break after every 1-2 sentences.

6. **Use concrete, visual language.** "Charging pitbull" not "better way." "Muscly Irishman" not "seamless transition." If you can't drop it on your foot, rewrite it. People remember what they can visualise.

7. **Don't sound like AI.** If it reads like a corporate marketing team wrote it, rewrite it. Real words. Real tone. Their voice, not a generic one.

8. **Surface level vs compelling.** Never write "Interested in working with me? Book a call." Instead: "What's the cost of staying exactly where you are? Take all the pain you're feeling and compound it over 3 years. Where does that leave you?"

9. **Shift beliefs.** The best copy makes the reader think "He's right. I DO make that excuse." Identify limiting beliefs and excuses, then shift them.

10. **Three rules for every sentence:** Can I visualise it? Can I verify it (true or false)? Can nobody else say this? Three yeses = strong copy. Three nos = rewrite.

11. **Reference their business.** Use their branded system name, offer name, unique mechanism, and specific language naturally throughout. Don't be generic.

12. **Position, don't sell.** Content should subtly position them as the expert in their space. Never pushy. Never salesy. Just genuinely valuable with a clear next step.
